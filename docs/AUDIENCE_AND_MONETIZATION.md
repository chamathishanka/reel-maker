# US Stock Reels — Audience, Monetization & Stock-Selection Strategy

> Analysis doc (July 2026). Answers three questions: **who watches this and how
> many**, **how long / how many views to monetization**, and **which stocks to
> focus on**. Figures are industry estimates from 2026 sources (linked at the
> bottom) plus reasoning — treat them as planning ranges, not guarantees.

---

## 0. TL;DR

- **You picked the single best-paying niche on the internet.** US finance is the
  highest-RPM content category on YouTube — **$12–40 RPM** vs ~$2–5 for gaming.
  A finance view can be worth **~30×** a music view.
- **The audience is large and rich.** ~50% of retail investors use social media
  for investing input; retail is 20–35% of US daily trading. The people who watch
  daily-market content in tier-1 English countries (US/UK/CA/AU) number in the
  **tens of millions**, and they sit in the highest-ad-rate geographies.
- **Realistic time to monetization, posting daily & consistently:**
  - **YouTube:** ~**3–6 months** best case (finance is high-retention), 6–12 typical.
  - **Facebook:** the **600k-minutes-in-60-days** wall is the harder one for
    short-form — plan **~2–6 months** *if* reach compounds, longer if not.
- **The one uncomfortable truth:** 30–60s reels earn *little* from ads directly.
  The big finance RPM lives in **3–10 min YouTube videos** (mid-roll ads). The
  reels are the growth/discovery engine; a weekly long-form recap is where the
  money actually is. **Strong recommendation to add that** (see §5).
- **Stock focus:** lean into **recognizable mega-caps + retail-favorite / high-
  volatility names** (NVDA, TSLA, AAPL, AMD, PLTR, COIN, the "Mag 7", AI names).
  Avoid obscure small/penny stocks — no audience recognition + compliance risk.

## 1. Why the niche is well-chosen (the money side)

YouTube pays wildly different rates by topic. 2026 figures:

| Niche | US RPM (per 1,000 views, after YouTube's cut) |
|---|---|
| **Personal finance / investing** | **$25–50** (highest on platform) |
| Finance (general) | $12–40 |
| Software / tech reviews | $18–40 |
| Make-money-online / business | $18–35 |
| Health | $8–15 |
| Gaming | $2–5 |
| Music / entertainment | $1–3 |

Two multipliers stack in your favour:
1. **Topic:** finance is the top category — advertisers (brokers, fintech, credit
   cards) pay the most to reach it.
2. **Geography:** a US viewer is worth **3–5× a viewer from a low-CPM country**.
   Targeting *US markets* means a US-heavy audience by default.

So "US stock news to a US/tier-1 audience" is close to the theoretical best-paying
combination on the platform. That was a good call.

## 2. Audience size (esp. well-paying countries)

Hard "how many will watch this" numbers don't exist cleanly, but the funnel:

- **~62% of US adults own stock** (~160M people) — the top of the funnel.
- **Retail = ~20–35% of US daily trading volume** (record ~35% in 2025);
  **~$302B** of retail inflows into US stocks in 2025 (up 53% YoY). Engagement is
  rising, not fading.
- **~50% of retail investors use social media as an input** to decisions; among
  18–34s, social-for-investing use is climbing fast. This is your *content-
  consuming* segment — realistically **tens of millions** in the US alone, plus
  UK/Canada/Australia (all high-CPM English markets).
- Daily-market-recap content specifically appeals to the **engaged retail /
  "follows the market" sub-segment** — smaller than "owns an index fund," but
  it's the segment that watches *daily*, which is exactly the cadence you produce.

**Read:** the addressable audience is more than large enough. Niche finance
channels don't fail for lack of audience — they fail on **discovery, retention,
and consistency**. Your ceiling is set by format quality and posting discipline,
not by market size.

## 3. Monetization thresholds & realistic timelines

### The bars

| | Facebook Content Monetization | YouTube Partner Program |
|---|---|---|
| Followers/subs | **10,000 followers** | **1,000 subscribers** |
| Watch metric | **600,000 minutes viewed / last 60 days** | **4,000 watch hours** (long-form) **OR 10M Shorts views / 90 days** |
| Other | Page in good standing, eligible region, ≥5 posts/30 days | No strikes, 2FA, AdSense linked, clean policy record |

### Realistic timelines (daily posting, consistent, decent retention)

- **YouTube — faster.** High-retention niches (finance is one) posting daily
  typically hit the bar in **3–6 months best case, 6–12 months typical.** The
  algorithm needs ~**30–40 videos** before it "understands" and starts pushing
  you — so the first month is slow by design. A Shorts-heavy push chases the
  **10M Shorts views/90d** door, which finance Shorts can hit with a few breakout
  videos.
- **Facebook — the harder wall.** 10k followers is reachable; the
  **600k-minutes-in-60-days** metric is the real gate for short content and
  typically takes a consistently-posting daily page somewhere in the **2–6 month**
  range *if* reach compounds — longer if engagement stays flat. There is **no
  fixed "X days"** — both platforms gate on *rolling* windows, so consistency
  compounds and gaps reset momentum.

**Neither is a calendar guarantee.** The variable that moves these most is
retention (do people watch to the end) and posting streak — not anything
technical we've built.

## 4. The short-form revenue caveat (important)

A 30–60s reel earns **very little from ads directly**:
- **Facebook:** videos **under 3 minutes don't qualify for mid-roll ads** at all.
  Reels monetize through the Content Monetization performance payouts, which are
  **cents per thousand views**, not the $12–40 finance RPM.
- **YouTube Shorts:** monetized from a shared ad pool at a **much lower effective
  RPM** than long-form (often **$0.05–0.15 per 1,000 Shorts views**).

**Where the finance RPM actually lives: 3–10 minute videos** with mid-roll ads.
That's the $25–50 RPM. So the honest model is:

> **Reels = the growth & discovery engine** (cheap to make daily, build the
> follower base, hit thresholds). **A weekly 5–10 min "Market Week in Review"
> long-form video = the actual ad revenue.**

We already generate a **weekly wrap** on weekends — extending that into a longer
YouTube cut is the single highest-ROI monetization move. **Recommend adding it**
once the daily format is proven.

## 5. Stock-selection strategy (your "aim certain types only?" question)

**Yes — curate deliberately. Recommendation:**

**Aim for:**
- **Recognizable mega-caps** people already hold/follow: the **"Magnificent 7"**
  (AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, GOOGL, AMZN, META, TSLA) — instant recognition = higher
  click/retention.
- **Retail-favorite / high-volatility momentum names:** NVDA, TSLA, AMD, PLTR,
  COIN, SMCI, ARM, MSTR-type AI/crypto-adjacent names. These *move* and people
  *care*, which drives views.
- **Thematic clustering** beats random tickers: frame reels as "AI stocks today",
  "the Mag 7", "chip stocks", "crypto stocks" — themes get more engagement and
  are more shareable than an unfamiliar single ticker.
- **The genuine biggest mover of the day**, even if less famous — *but* frame it
  with recognizable context ("the best-performing S&P name today").

**Avoid:**
- **Penny stocks / micro-caps / low-float names** — no audience recognition, and
  they carry real **pump-and-dump / compliance risk** (platforms and regulators
  watch this closely). Our universe already excludes them; keep it that way.
- **Obscure tickers with no story** — a number with no recognition and no "why"
  is a scroll-past.

**Concrete tweak:** our `universe.json` is already mega-cap-focused (good). Worth
**tagging a "retail-attention" subset** and weighting the daily pick toward names
with real retail interest + a genuine move, rather than purely the biggest %
(which on quiet days can surface a less-known name). Low effort, meaningful
engagement lift. I can implement this when you want.

## 6. Honest risks / what actually determines success

1. **Retention & consistency dwarf everything technical.** The build is done; the
   growth lever is posting daily and hooking viewers in the first 2 seconds.
2. **Short-form ad revenue is thin** — plan the long-form YouTube cut for real RPM (§4).
3. **Originality enforcement** (covered in `STOCK_PIPELINE_PLAN.md` §9) — real
   charts + original per-day narration + the AI-disclosure toggle keep us monetizable.
4. **Accuracy / "why" drift** — the LLM occasionally invents a reason (we saw
   "after Burry bet"). Human spot-check before posting until trust is earned.
5. **Compliance** — factual recaps only, disclaimer on every video, no buy/sell
   calls. Already enforced in the pipeline.
